
# Honeypot Attack Summary Report

## 1. Report Information

*   **Report ID:** HSR-20250928-001
*   **Date of Report:** 2025-09-28T22-01-20Z
*   **Reporting Period:** 2025-09-28T21:20:01Z to 2025-09-28T22:00:01Z
*   **Analyst:** Cybersecurity Analyst

## 2. Executive Summary

This report summarizes the analysis of 14,984 malicious events captured by our distributed honeypot network over a 40-minute period on September 28, 2025. The data reveals a high volume of automated attacks, primarily targeting SSH services and leveraging common vulnerabilities.

Key findings include:
*   **Total Events:** A total of 14,984 suspicious events were logged across all honeypots.
*   **Most Active Honeypot:** The `Cowrie` honeypot, emulating an SSH server, was the most targeted, accounting for 7,241 (48.3%) of all interactions.
*   **Top Attacker IP:** The IP address `164.92.85.77` was the most prolific attacker, responsible for 1,247 events, primarily targeting the Cowrie honeypot.
*   **Most Targeted Port:** Port `5038` (Asterisk Manager Interface) was the most frequently attacked port, indicating interest in VoIP-related services. Port `22` (SSH) was the second most targeted.
*   **Vulnerability Exploitation:** Several CVEs were targeted, with `CVE-2022-27255` (a remote code execution vulnerability in `init` running on MIPS-based routers) being the most frequently exploited.
*   **Brute-Force Activity:** A significant number of brute-force attempts were observed, with `345gs5662d34/345gs5662d34` being the most commonly used credential pair.

The threat landscape continues to be dominated by automated scanning and exploitation attempts. The high volume of attacks on SSH and VoIP services suggests that attackers are actively seeking to compromise these systems for botnet recruitment, credential harvesting, or to establish persistent access. The observed commands indicate post-exploitation activities focused on reconnaissance and establishing backdoors.
